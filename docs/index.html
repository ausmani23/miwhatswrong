The Inefficiency of Mass Incarceration

Explore trade-offs in criminal justice policy. How do changes in policing and incarceration affect overall welfare? Based on What's Wrong with Mass Incarceration? by Christopher Lewis & Adaner Usmani.

This calculator estimates the net welfare effect of shifting U.S. police and prison rates from their current levels. Use the Policy Choices sliders on the left to set a proposed police and prison rate, then examine the heatmap and breakdown on the right. The heatmap shows how net welfare varies across the full policy space; the white star marks the optimal point. All estimates depend on assumptions about how police and prisons affect crime, and how to weigh different harms. You can adjust these under Assumptions below. Every parameter has a default grounded in the empirical literature, but you can explore how conclusions change under alternative values.
